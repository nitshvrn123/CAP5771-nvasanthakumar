DIARY ENTRY: REFLECTION AND NEXT STEPS

PROJECT REFLECTION

Milestone 1 focused on establishing a strong analytical foundation before moving into predictive modeling. The primary goal was to clearly define a measurable research question and ensure that the data acquisition, storage, and exploration stages directly support that question.

The project evolved from a general idea about cricket performance into a structured investigation of performance risk under competitive pressure. A key decision was to operationalize pressure using tournament stage (league, playoffs, final). This provides an objective and reproducible measure of competitive intensity without relying on subjective interpretation.

Designing the relational SQLite database was an important step. Separating match metadata from player-level batting and bowling tables ensured clarity and interpretability. This structure also supports reproducibility, as all analysis can be recreated directly from the database using SQL queries and pandas.

During exploratory analysis, I verified:

• The dataset is structurally consistent and complete.
• Missing values are mostly structural (e.g., bowling strike rate when no wicket is taken).
• League matches dominate the dataset, while finals represent a smaller subset.
• Extreme performances are meaningful and relevant for studying risk and variability.

No unnecessary transformations were applied during exploration. Instead, potential issues were documented to guide future modeling decisions.

KEY LEARNINGS

This milestone reinforced several important data science principles:

• A clearly defined problem statement is critical before analysis begins.
• Database design should align directly with analytical goals.
• Risk and variability provide deeper insight than averages alone.
• Reproducibility and documentation are essential for professional work.

The process emphasized structured thinking, clean organization, and transparency.

NEXT STEPS

The next phase of the project will extend this framework into predictive modeling.

1) Machine Learning Models

Batting Model:
Target: Runs scored in a match  
Features:
• Historical average runs  
• Variance of runs  
• Low-score rate  
• Match stage  
• Season  
• Venue  

Bowling Model:
Target: Economy rate per match  
Features:
• Historical average economy  
• Wicket rate  
• Dot-ball rate  
• Match stage  
• Season  
• Venue  

All features will be computed using only prior matches to prevent data leakage.

2) Model Evaluation

• Train-test split or time-based validation
• Cross-validation
• Comparison of prediction error across match stages
• Analysis of whether performance becomes less predictable under pressure

3) Advanced Risk Analysis

• Refinement of Pressure Impact Indices
• Uncertainty quantification
• Interaction effects between stage and venue
• Identification of pressure-resilient vs pressure-sensitive players

FINAL THOUGHT

Milestone 1 successfully establishes a clean, structured, and reproducible framework for analyzing performance risk in professional cricket. The database schema, documentation, and exploratory analysis provide a strong foundation for advanced modeling and deeper stage-aware performance analysis in future milestones.
