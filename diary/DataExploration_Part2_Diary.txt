DIARY ENTRY: DATA EXPLORATION — PART II (ISSUE IDENTIFICATION)

This section documents potential data issues identified during exploratory analysis. 
The objective at this stage is not to resolve these issues, but to clearly record and understand them before proceeding to modeling.

⸻

DUPLICATES AND UNIQUENESS

No exact duplicate records were observed in either the batting or bowling datasets.

Although no explicit primary key constraints are enforced in SQLite for the performance tables, player–match records are effectively unique based on:

- (match_id, player) for batting
- (match_id, bowler) for bowling

This definition of uniqueness aligns with the analytical objective of studying performance at the player–match level.

⸻

MISSING VALUES

Missing values were observed primarily in bowling-related metrics such as strike_rate_balls_per_wicket.

These missing values are structural rather than errors. For example:
- If a bowler takes zero wickets in a match, bowling strike rate (balls per wicket) is undefined.
- Such cases reflect cricket rules and performance mechanics, not data corruption.

Missingness in batting variables was minimal after filtering to relevant match stages.

No imputation was performed at this stage.

⸻

STANDARDIZATION AND DATA TYPES

Match stages were standardized using the `stage_bucket` variable, categorizing matches into:
- league
- playoffs
- final

After filtering, no inconsistent or unexpected stage labels were detected.

All numeric performance variables (runs, economy, wickets, etc.) were verified to have appropriate numeric data types.

⸻

NOISE VS OUTLIERS

Extreme performances (e.g., unusually high scores or expensive bowling spells) were retained.

Because the project focuses on performance risk and variability, these extreme outcomes are treated as informative signals rather than noise to be removed.

Final-stage matches are relatively rare, introducing greater statistical uncertainty for that stage. This imbalance is documented and will be handled analytically rather than corrected during exploration.

⸻

OPEN QUESTIONS FOR FUTURE CONSIDERATION

Several design decisions may require clarification:

- Should abandoned or no-result matches be excluded from risk analysis?
- Is the current grouping of qualifiers and eliminators into the playoffs category appropriate?
- Should stricter minimum appearance thresholds be enforced for finals due to smaller sample sizes?
- Are super-over performances included, and if so, should they be treated differently?

These questions are documented for transparency and potential refinement in future milestones.

⸻

SUMMARY

The dataset appears structurally sound, with no duplicate records and only expected structural missingness. 
All identified issues have been documented to ensure methodological transparency before advancing to modeling and pressure index construction.
