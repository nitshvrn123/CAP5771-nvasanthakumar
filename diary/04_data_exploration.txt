DIARY ENTRY: DATA EXPLORATION

At this stage of the project, exploratory analysis was conducted to understand the structure,
content, and limitations of the IPL performance dataset before any modeling or data
transformation.

The exploration focused on player–match level data for batting and bowling, filtered to
the three tournament stages relevant to the study: league, playoffs, and finals. Only
features necessary for stage-aware performance risk analysis were retained.

DATA PREVIEW

The dataset was previewed using standard inspection methods (.head(), .info(), .describe())
directly in the project notebook. This confirmed that batting and bowling performance metrics
were stored at the appropriate granularity, with numeric fields such as runs, balls, economy,
and wickets showing reasonable ranges and distributions.

Stage distribution counts revealed a strong imbalance across match stages, with league matches
comprising the majority of observations and finals representing a much smaller subset. This
imbalance was noted as an important consideration for interpretation rather than corrected
during exploration.

ISSUE IDENTIFICATION

Several potential data issues were identified and documented:

• Duplicate records were checked and none were found. Player–match records are effectively
  unique based on the combination of match_id and player (for batting) or match_id and bowler
  (for bowling).

• Missing values were observed primarily in bowling-related metrics such as strike rate per
  wicket. These occur when a bowler does not take a wicket in a match and reflect structural
  characteristics of cricket data rather than data quality errors.

• Stage labels were standardized using the stage_bucket variable, which cleanly categorizes
  matches into league, playoffs, and final. No inconsistent categories were observed after
  filtering.

• Extreme performances (e.g., very high scores or expensive bowling spells) were retained.
  Because the project focuses on performance risk and variability, such extremes are treated
  as informative signals rather than noise.

No data issues were corrected at this stage. All findings were recorded to guide future data
wrangling and modeling decisions.

The project notebook serves as the primary and authoritative record of exploratory analysis,
including descriptive statistics, missingness reports, and visual summaries.
